# PPO_23 Design: Longer Rollouts (n_steps=4096)

_Date: 2026-02-27_

## Goal

Break the eval reward plateau (~22) seen across PPO_16–22 by improving credit assignment for delayed combo rewards.

## Root Cause Targeted

With `n_steps=2048` and `gae_lambda=0.90`, advantages decay to `0.90^30 ≈ 0.04` after 30 steps. Combo chains that require 5+ block placements to score get almost no gradient signal — the policy can't learn that early placements caused a later sweep reward.

## Change

**Single line change in `python/train.py`:**

```python
# Before (PPO_22)
n_steps=2048,

# After (PPO_23)
n_steps=4096,
```

No other changes.

## Side Effects

| Property | PPO_22 | PPO_23 |
|----------|--------|--------|
| Buffer per update | 2048 × 16 = 32,768 | 4096 × 16 = 65,536 |
| Minibatches per update (batch_size=256) | 128 | 256 |
| Policy update frequency | Every 32,768 env steps | Every 65,536 env steps |
| Eval frequency | Every 50,000 total steps | Every 50,000 total steps (unchanged) |

- `batch_size=256` divides evenly into 65,536 → no issue
- Policy updates less frequently but on richer, longer trajectories — intended tradeoff
- All other hyperparameters unchanged (LR schedule, entropy, gamma, gae_lambda, etc.)

## Success Criteria

- Eval reward breaks above 24 (current best)
- EV stays ≥ 0.65 (critic keeping up with longer rollouts)
- No KL explosion (target_kl=0.008 guard still in place)
